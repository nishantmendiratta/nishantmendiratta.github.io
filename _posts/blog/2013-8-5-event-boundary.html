"I came in here for something, but I can't remember what it is ..."

If you've ever said something like this, you've probably experienced an "event boundary."

The University of Notre Dame in Indiana conducted a study on this, concluding that walking through doorways causes memory to lapse.

"Entering or exiting through a doorway serves as an 'event boundary' in the mind, which separates episodes of activity and files them away." - As researcher Gabriel Radvansky explained.
